# 4.7 Fairness, Robustness, and Regulatory Compliance

## Thesis (one sentence)

Fairness in credit scoring is operationalised through four mathematically incompatible parity definitions (statistical, equalised odds, predictive, calibration-within-group), forcing modellers to choose which fits the deployment context — and the EU AI Act, FCA Consumer Duty, and EBA loan-origination guidelines now make this choice an auditable compliance obligation rather than an academic preference.

## Argumentative arc

1. **Four parity definitions, mathematically incompatible.** Statistical parity, equalised odds [[hardt2016eqodds]], predictive parity, calibration within groups — provably incompatible when base rates differ across groups [[chouldechova2017fair]], [[kleinberg2017inherent]]. The choice of which to optimise is a value judgement, not a technical one.
2. **Proxy attributes for green lending.** Protected attributes (race, gender) are not in our dataset, but Industry, Borrower State, and business-size segments are tested as proxy axes for indirect disparate impact (ECOA / UK equivalent).
3. **Operational toolkit.** AIF360 [[bellamy2018aif360]] implements the parity metrics + mitigation (reweighting [[kamiran2012preprocessing]], adversarial debiasing [[zhang2018adversarial]]).
4. **Regulatory triggers.** EU AI Act [[euAiAct2021]] classifies credit scoring as Annex III high-risk, triggering obligations across Articles 9–15 (data governance, technical documentation, transparency, human oversight, accuracy/robustness logging, post-market monitoring). FCA Consumer Duty [[fcaConsumerDuty2022]] adds outcome-based fairness; EBA GL 2020/06 [[ebaGL2020]] requires demonstrable model robustness and explainability.
5. **Robustness beyond fairness.** Stability under input perturbation, adversarial input resistance, concept-drift monitoring [[lu2018conceptdrift]] are all required for high-risk AI under Article 15 of the EU AI Act.

## Counter-evidence to keep honest

- Industry and state may not be valid "protected proxy" axes under ECOA — they typically aren't directly protected. Frame this as "indirect disparate impact testing", not "protected class compliance".
- The four-parity-incompatibility result has been challenged in cases where base rates can be made equal through intervention. Worth a footnote.
- Adversarial debiasing can degrade utility substantially; the cost-benefit trade-off is application-specific.

## Open holes

- No explicit reference to UK Equality Act 2010 or the FCA Handbook's existing fair-treatment-of-customers rules pre-Consumer Duty.
- No mention of recent Basel "model risk management" guidance or US SR 11-7 — would strengthen the regulatory positioning.
- Causal fairness (Kusner et al. 2017 counterfactual fairness) is missing entirely. Could deserve a paragraph if reviewers push on it.

## Drafted prose (currently in proposal_second_draft.docx)

> Fairness in credit scoring is operationalised through statistical parity, equalised odds [Hardt et al., 2016], predictive parity, and calibration within groups — definitions that are provably incompatible in non-trivial cases [Chouldechova, 2017; Kleinberg et al., 2017], forcing modellers to choose which parity criterion best fits the deployment context. In the green lending case, protected attributes (race, gender) are not directly available, but proxies (industry, geography, business size) can produce indirect disparate impact testable under ECOA and equivalent UK regulation. The EU AI Act [European Commission, 2021] classifies credit scoring as Annex III high-risk, triggering obligations around data governance, technical documentation, human oversight, accuracy and robustness logging, and post-market monitoring; the FCA Consumer Duty [FCA, 2022] adds outcome-based fairness obligations; and the EBA Guidelines on Loan Origination and Monitoring [EBA, 2020] require lenders to demonstrate model robustness and explainability. Robustness extends beyond fairness to stability under input perturbation, adversarial input resistance, and concept-drift monitoring [Lu et al., 2018]. EMERALD-AI integrates these audits into the development loop rather than treating them as post-hoc checklists.
