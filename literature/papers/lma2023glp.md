        # Loan Market Association, APLMA & LSTA (2023) — Green Loan Principles

        ## Key claims
        - Defines four pillars for green-loan governance: use of proceeds, process for project evaluation and selection, management of proceeds, and reporting.
- Voluntary, market-led standard; widely adopted by syndicated-loan participants but not legally binding.

        ## Method (how the claim was established)
        Industry consensus document maintained jointly by LMA, APLMA, and LSTA.

        ## Relevance to EMERALD-AI
        Authority on what 'green loan' means in practice. Useful framing in introduction and section 4.5. The 'use of proceeds' pillar is the closest thing to a definition we have for the dataset's labelling.

        ## Quotable lines
        - The four-pillar structure is the standard citable reference for green-loan governance.

        ## Limitations / counter-evidence
        - Voluntary; compliance is not externally audited.
- Project-evaluation criteria are intentionally non-prescriptive, leaving wide variance across lenders.
- Updated periodically; ensure citation uses the current (Feb 2023) version.

        ## How EMERALD-AI uses this paper
        Introduction (market growth claim). Section 4.5 (governance framing). The dataset's 'green' provenance may or may not align with GLP — flagged as a known data-governance question in [[gaps]].
