# Ehlers & Packer (2017) — BIS Quarterly Review on information asymmetry in green debt

## Key claims
- The green-debt market is characterised by significant information asymmetry between issuers and investors over the genuine environmental quality of funded projects.
- Third-party certification (Climate Bonds Initiative, second-party opinions) partially mitigates but does not fully resolve the asymmetry.
- Greenwashing — labelling debt as green without genuine eligibility — is a structural risk for the asset class.

## Method (how the claim was established)
Descriptive analysis of the global green-bond market and the certification ecosystem, with case studies.

## Relevance to EMERALD-AI
Authority citation for section 4.5's greenwashing argument and for the introduction's framing of structural information gaps in green lending.

## Quotable lines
- 'Green bond finance and certification: third parties verify that the proceeds will fund genuinely environmental projects.'
- The information-asymmetry framing is the most-cited contribution.

## Limitations / counter-evidence
- Pre-dates the EU Taxonomy Regulation and SFDR; regulatory landscape has evolved.
- Focus is on green bonds, not green loans — the structural argument transfers but with caveats.
